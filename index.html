Helping combat veterans fight their hardest battle: coming home.

Our Heroes exists for one mission: to walk beside combat veterans as they fight through the invisible war that starts after the deployments end. We know the cost of service isn’t paid off when the uniform comes off. For many, that’s when the hardest chapter begins.

My name is Dominic King. I served nine years in the United States Marine Corps and deployed four times in support of Operation Enduring Freedom. The veteran I am trying to help is the man I used to be.

When I came home, I didn’t “fit” anywhere. Rage with no clear target. A constant pull toward self-destruction. I turned to alcohol and substances not because I wanted to party, but because I wanted to go numb. Starting over in the civilian world after years in combat felt like being dropped on a different planet and told, “Figure it out.”

By the grace of God, some hard decisions, and a new sense of direction, my story didn’t end there. I found my footing. I built a family I adore. I rebuilt a version of myself that I can be proud of. But it was almost an accident that I made it back.

That is why Our Heroes exists — so that “happenchance” isn’t the plan. We want to call out the elephant in the room: substance abuse, isolation, the feeling of being misunderstood, the fear of starting at the bottom again. And then we want to do something about it.

Veterans We Serve

Our focus is on combat veterans who feel like the world they returned to doesn’t match the world they left. If any of this sounds familiar, you are exactly who we exist to serve:

  • You feel like you don’t quite fit anywhere — not at work, not in crowds, sometimes not even at home.
  • You’ve leaned on alcohol or substances just to slow your thoughts down or feel something different.
  • Going “back to the bottom” in a civilian job feels insulting after what you’ve already done.
  • You’re carrying things you don’t know how to explain, and you’re tired of people saying, “Thank you for your service,” but not really understanding you.
  • You want a path forward that doesn’t erase your military background, but builds on it.

We don’t treat you like a problem that needs to be fixed. We see you as a leader who has already proven what you’re capable of — you just need the right direction, the right support, and a team that understands the terrain.

Re-entry & Employment Coaching

Help translating your MOS and combat experience into real-world skills, building a new career path, and navigating the humility of starting over without losing your identity.

Substance & Coping Support

Guidance toward healthy resources for dealing with substance abuse and destructive habits — not with shame, but with honesty and a plan.

Connection & Community

Linking veterans with others who get it — people who have walked the same road, felt the same anger, and decided they were not going to let it write the ending of their story.

Application for Help

If you’re reading this and thinking, “That sounds like me,” you are exactly the person this foundation was built for. Reaching out is not weakness — it is the same courage you showed downrange, pointed in a new direction.

This simple form is here so we can start a conversation. We’re not asking you to spill everything on day one. Just give us a way to reach you and a glimpse of what you’re facing.

If forms aren’t your thing, you can email us directly at info@our-heroes.org.
